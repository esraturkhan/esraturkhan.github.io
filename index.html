VISIT ALMERE

Welcome to Almere. Alongside the striking modern architecture and outdoor artworks, Almere Centrum has more than 400 shops, a varied cultural agenda and cinemas. The natural surroundings also attract visitors, who come to admire the unique 'polder' landscapes, sail one of the lakes or simply relax in the green open spaces. The Oostvaardersplassen nature reserve is a particular highlight for hiking, cycling and spotting wildlife.

24 Hours in Almere

We are happy to give you a number of tips to discover Almere in 24 hours. It is not long, but definitely worth it.

10 am

Start your day with coffee at MockaMore to relax and you are ready to start the day.

11 am

With more than 440 km of free cycle paths, the ultimate way to discover Almere by bike. To rent a (electric) bike, head for the VVV Almere Tourist Info, where you will find the best bikes at a competitive rate

12.30 pm

Got hungry? Time to have a nice lunch at Boathouse. If the weather is nice, we recommend a spot on the terrace, you have a wonderful view over the Noorderplassen.

2 pm

Time to leave again. We cycle back to the Almere's city centre. By the way, did you know the has been named Best City Centre 2017 - 2020 of The Nederlands?

3:30 pm

Bring your bike back to the VVV Almere Tourist Info and take a nice stroll through the center of Almere. Want to view Almere in LEGO bricks? Vamos a Almere

Did you know that Almere boasts a coastline of over 42 kilometres and has the largest catamaran beach in Europe? Located at the Muiderzand Marina this beach is also an official kite-surfing location. The long coastline means a wealth of wonderful beaches for playing, relax or taking a dip. Young and old, big and small, Almere's beaches are freely accessible to all. Grab your bike, take the car or board a bus or train and make for the beach.
